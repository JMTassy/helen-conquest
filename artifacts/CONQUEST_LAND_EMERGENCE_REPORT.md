## Mechanism: Stigmergy & Collaborative Clustering

### What is Stigmergy?

Stigmergy is indirect coordination through environmental traces. In this simulation:

1. **Agent A performs task** → leaves trace (quality score, speed score, collaborators)
2. **Agent B observes trace** → adjusts collaboration preference
3. **Over iterations** → preferred partners cluster together
4. **Cluster stabilizes** → becomes a House with persistent identity

## Connection to Agent Governance Research

This simulation demonstrates principles found in modern multi-agent systems literature:

### 1. **Consensus Dynamics** (Olfati-Saber et al.)
Agents with local interaction rules converge to system-level consensus on strategy.

### 2. **Stigmergy** (Theraulaz & Bonabeau)
Indirect coordination through environmental traces (ledger traces in our case) enables emergent structure without central authority.

### 3. **Specialization in Networks** (Barabási)
Complex networks naturally develop specialist and generalist nodes based on interaction patterns.

### 4. **Collective Intelligence** (Surowiecki)
Diverse agents (Speed, Accuracy, Balance) combined with **independent** judgment (each agent decides collaborators) produce better decisions than any single agent.
