## 3️⃣ Counter-Schema: How This System Would Fail

**This is the most important part.**

You don't protect a system by praising it.
You protect it by recognizing its death patterns early.

---

### ❌ Failure Mode A — Soft Normativity

**What it looks like:**
- "Interesting that Porto succeeded with…"
- "Many towns are converging on…"
- "This pattern suggests…"

**Why it's lethal:**
- Norms sneak in before authority
- Imitation begins without being named

**Early Warning Signal:**
- NPC language shifts from descriptive → evaluative
- Adverbs appear: notably, remarkably, surprisingly

**Hard Rule:**
> The moment insight sounds wise, it is suspect.

---

### ❌ Failure Mode B — Insight Prestige

**What it looks like:**
- Certain NPCs quoted more often
- Certain metaphors repeated
- Certain voices "waited for"

**Why it's lethal:**
- Authority relocates from kernel → culture
- The system obeys without deciding

**Early Warning Signal:**
- NPC names remembered more than observations
- "As X said in Calvi last year…"

**Hard Rule:**
> No insight is ever allowed to become a reference.

---

### ❌ Failure Mode C — Metric Seduction

**What it looks like:**
- SHIP rate celebrated
- Low override count praised
- Stability framed as success

**Why it's lethal:**
- Optimization pressure replaces judgment
- The system begins to please itself

**Early Warning Signal:**
- People feel bad on "bad metric days"
- Good decisions feel wrong if numbers dip

**Hard Rule:**
> Metrics may explain the past, never justify the present.

---

### ❌ Failure Mode D — Ritual Inflation (Davos Drift)

**What it looks like:**
- CALVI becomes prestigious
- Attendance becomes status
- Speeches sound "important"

**Why it's lethal:**
- The agora turns into a stage
- Silence becomes suspicious

**Early Warning Signal:**
- Applause replaces pauses
- Panels replace walks
- Conclusions appear

**Hard Rule:**
> If Calvi ever produces a decision, Calvi is dead.

---

### ❌ Failure Mode E — Silent Prestige Transfer

**What it looks like:**
- Porto's judgments adopted subconsciously
- No explicit "this is right" — just subtle behavioral shift
- Other towns copying without naming it

**Why it's lethal:**
- Authority doesn't announce itself
- Dialectal Sovereignty collapses before parameters do

**Early Warning Signal:**
- NPC word choice becomes more Porto-like
- Parameter changes without amendment
- Town boundaries feel softer

**Hard Rule:**
> If language converges before parameters do, the culture is already gone.

---

### ❌ Failure Mode F — Authority Latency

**What it looks like:**
- Decisions shift subtly over months
- No single point of corruption
- System gradually "learns" which answers are safer

**Why it's lethal:**
- K0 (authority separation) dies slowly
- By the time you notice, the kernel is already contaminated

**Early Warning Signal:**
- Override patterns shift (small, consistent direction)
- Refusal reasons become repetitive ("too similar to X")
- Claims start pre-filtered by NPCs

**Hard Rule:**
> A tiny drift in the wrong direction is worse than a large, visible failure.

## Core Truths (Memorize These)

1. **Refusal is not failure — it is clarity**
   - Accepting everything is easier than deciding well
   - If SHIP rates climb, suspect laziness

2. **Silence is not stagnation — it is data**
   - Boredom is a signal, not a problem
   - If you feel restless, the system is working

3. **Small drifts kill systems faster than large failures**
   - Watch for 1% shifts, not 50% breaks
   - The system dies quietly, not with warning

4. **Authority does not announce itself**
   - Corruption looks like helpfulness
   - By the time you see it, it is already invisible

5. **Prestige is the mother of imitation**
   - Keep Porto known but never praised
   - Success must remain visible but tasteless

If you inherit this system:

## Mythic Frame (Culture-Safe, Founder-Neutral)

**Why Calvi Exists (The Story)**

Long ago, when the three towns had grown too proud of their own silence, they gathered in Calvi, on the rocks above the sea.

They did not come to decide. They came to remember.

The NPCs spoke first — not to counsel, but to name what they had seen. Metaphors emerged. Questions hung in the air like sea salt.

Then the Mayors rose, not to agree, but to witness.

No consensus was reached. No conclusions were drawn. No decisions were made.

But something had happened: the silence had been made sacred.

And when they returned to their towns, they remembered that intelligence without restraint is only noise.

They remembered that refusal is not cruelty — it is care.

They remembered that the system did not serve them. They served the system.

Every year since, they gather again in Calvi.
Not to solve.
Not to improve.
Only to remember.
