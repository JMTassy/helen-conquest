## How Constitutional Governance Attracts Culture

### Mechanism 1: No Reputation Cashing

Traditional systems:
- Trusted actors get expedited review
- Creates two-tier culture (insiders vs. outsiders)
- Authority inflates over time (past wins compound)
- Corruption enters through trusted channels

Oracle Town:
- Every claim faces identical cryptographic scrutiny
- No special status for known actors
- Credibility earned fresh each time
- No authority inflation possible

**Result:** No reputation to lean on, so people focus on *this specific claim*. Integrity preserved.

### Mechanism 2: Fail-Closed by Default

Traditional systems:
- Ambiguity → Approve (trust the proposer)
- Incomplete evidence → Accept (assume good intent)
- Uncertainty → Move forward (bias toward action)

Oracle Town:
- Ambiguity → NO_SHIP (require clarity)
- Incomplete evidence → NO_SHIP (require completeness)
- Uncertainty → NO_SHIP (require confidence)

**Result:** People stop relying on assumptions, start documenting everything. Honesty emerges.

### Mechanism 3: Receipts Replace Reputation

Traditional authority:
- "Trust me because I said so"
- "I've always been right"
- "Experts agree"

Oracle Town:
- "Here's exactly what I claim, hashed for proof"
- "You can replay this identically"
- "Tribunal decides, not me"

**Result:** No one leans on authority, everyone documents rigorously. Transparency becomes self-defense.

### Mechanism 4: Diversity Is Built In

Traditional systems:
- Single decision-maker (CEO, board, expert)
- Creates single-point-of-view bias
- Missing perspectives go unnoticed

Oracle Town (K3 quorum-by-class):
- Multiple independent perspectives required
- Each prevents different blind spots
- Missing any perspective blocks decision

**Result:** No single view can dominate, diversity is enforced. Balance emerges.
