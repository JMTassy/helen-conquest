## 1. What the Science Actually Says

The sober core:

> **The brain is not a camera. It is a controlled hallucination engine
> constrained by sensory input, memory, body state, and prediction
> error.**

Standard claims from the published literature, used here as **design
inspiration**, not as actionable medical/spiritual claims:

- **Classical psychedelics** (DMT, psilocybin, LSD, mescaline) are mainly
  associated with **5-HT2A receptor agonism**. Effects span molecular
  signaling, cortical spiking dynamics, thalamocortical connectivity,
  association-cortex activity, and structural plasticity.
- A useful high-level model is **increased neural entropy** — psychedelics
  appear to increase the variability/entropy of spontaneous neural
  activity, possibly relating to the richness, instability, and novelty
  of psychedelic experience.
- Recent reviews emphasize **increased global integration and reduced
  network modularity**; disruption of **default-mode-network (DMN)**
  dynamics is often linked to ego-dissolution and altered self-modeling.
- **Salvia is mechanistically different** from classical serotonergic
  psychedelics. **Salvinorin A is a potent and selective kappa-opioid
  receptor agonist**, not a 5-HT2A agonist. Human studies report
  dissociative effects, altered interoception, reduced contact with
  external audiovisual reality, and disrupted body ownership.
- The **claustrum** has been proposed as a hub related to consciousness
  and integrative processing. The literature is **cautious and
  contested**. Salvia/claustrum hypotheses link kappa-opioid receptor
  localization and claustral disruption to unusual altered states, but
  this remains theory, not settled fact.
- **Ibogaine** is being investigated for substance-use disorders and is
  associated with powerful autobiographical / perspective-review
  experiences, but reviews stress fragmented evidence, regulatory gaps,
  and **serious cardiac safety concerns including QT/QTc prolongation
  and malignant arrhythmias**. **For LNOS: use only as metaphor for
  perspective review, never as recommendation.**

## 4. The Reality Renderer Loop — Five Experiments

Each experiment has a **hypothesis**, a **prototype**, a **metric**, and
an **explicit keep/discard rule**.

### Experiment 1 — Hyper-real does not mean true

- **Hypothesis:** if players see a "hyper-real" Akashic vision and then
  HAL labels it `VISION_LAYER`, they will better understand the LNOS
  truth boundary.
- **Prototype:** Akashic fragment appears with impossible geometry. HAL
  overlay: *"Intensity is not evidence."*
- **Metric:** can the player correctly classify a fragment as
  `VISION` / `MEMORY` / `CLAIM` / `RECEIPT`? **Target: > 80% after
  onboarding.**
- **Keep if:** players understand the boundary.
- **Discard if:** players think the game is asserting metaphysical truth.

### Experiment 2 — Objecthood Collapse

- **Hypothesis:** temporarily becoming an object teaches perspective
  instability better than exposition.
- **Prototype:** player becomes a page, chair, feather, stone brick, or
  queue ticket (per `#009`).
- **Metric:** does the player correctly answer *"What changed: reality
  itself, or your perspective anchor?"* **Target: > 75%.**
- **Keep if:** players cleanly separate perspective shift from cosmic
  claim.
- **Discard if:** players come away thinking the object is "really
  alive."

**HAL line:**
> *You are experiencing a perspective-anchor shift. Do not canonize
> objecthood.*

### Experiment 3 — Other-Side Mirror

- **Hypothesis:** a replay from another NPC's perspective creates
  empathy without requiring drug framing.
- **Prototype:** player relives a choice from the affected NPC's
  perspective (per `#008` §6, ibogaine-inspired *but no medical claim*).
- **Metric:** does the player select a repair action afterward? Does
  the player distinguish "symbolic perspective review" from factual
  memory?
- **Keep if:** repair-action selection rate is high and the
  symbolic/factual distinction holds.
- **Discard if:** players believe the replay is a literal historical
  fact about themselves.

### Experiment 4 — Tone-Fracture

- **Hypothesis:** sound + color tuning gives a safe, playable version of
  "frequency threshold" myths (per `#010`).
- **Prototype:** tone wheel stabilizes a fracture only when sound,
  color, and glyph align.
- **Metric:**
  - time-to-understand puzzle
  - false-belief risk (post-puzzle classification questions)
  - player can explain: *"tone is game mechanic, not physics claim."*
- **Keep if:** players can articulate the mechanic-vs-physics
  distinction unprompted.
- **Discard if:** players begin asserting real-world frequency claims
  outside the game.

### Experiment 5 — Akashic Receipt Gate

- **Hypothesis:** players will enjoy visions *more* if the game makes
  verification part of the mystery.
- **Prototype:** three glowing fragments appear. **Only one can be
  receipt-bound.** Two remain beautiful but non-canon.
- **Metric:** does the player accept leaving some visions unclaimed?
  **Target: > 60% without frustration.**
- **Keep if:** players come to value unclaimed beauty.
- **Discard if:** the unclaimed fragments cause persistent dissatisfaction.

This experiment is **extremely HELEN**:

> *A mature player can leave beauty unclaimed.*
