## 2. The Reality Engine (LNOS Lore)

> Every mind runs a renderer.
>
> Most days, the renderer is stable:
> objects stay objects,
> time moves forward,
> self feels continuous,
> the world behaves.
>
> But under certain conditions — sound, color, stress, dream, ritual,
> psychedelic noise, memory shock — the renderer **desynchronizes**.
>
> The world does not disappear. It becomes *editable*.
>
> Not literally. Not as doctrine. **As experience.**
>
> The Akashic Atrium is the place in LNOS where players learn this:
> what feels real is not always true,
> what is constructed is not always false,
> and what changes you must still be integrated carefully.

**Inputs that destabilize the renderer:**

**Effects:**

- NPC masks speak in archetypes

## 6. Quest Type — The Other-Side Mirror (Ibogaine-Inspired)

**This quest is inspired by reported ibogaine-experience phenomenology
(perspective-shifted memory review). It is not a drug-use quest. It is a
perspective-review trial in fictional gameplay.**

**Premise:** the player enters a chamber where they relive an old action,
but from the perspective of the person affected by it. This is **not
punishment.** It is perspective reconstruction.

**Objectives:**
1. Enter the Mirror Chamber.
2. Watch the memory from self-perspective.
3. Replay it from other-perspective.
4. Identify the harm, misunderstanding, or missing context.
5. Write a repair action.
6. Submit an integration receipt.

**Reward:** `COMPASSION_SIGNAL` unlocked.

**Core line:**
> *The roughest visions are not always enemies. Some are invoices from
> empathy.*
