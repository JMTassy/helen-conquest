## 3. Mechanic — Objecthood Collapse

**Triggers:**
- entering the Claustrum Gate
- touching corrupted Akashic pages
- unresolved identity conflict
- high symbolic overload

**Effect:** the player's point of view temporarily attaches to a
non-human object.

**Examples:**
- become a page in the Archive
- become a brick in a Shutu pyramid server
- become the Feather Antenna itself
- become the queue ticket held by House Rakham
- become a door that refuses false authority

**Purpose:** teach that perspective is *constructed*.

**Risk:** player mistakes intensity for truth.

**HAL safeguard:** the experience must end with classification and an
**integration receipt**. No persistent in-world change is admitted from
an Objecthood Collapse session without that receipt.

## 4. Mechanic — Book-Life Loop

The player opens an Akashic book.

Instead of *reading* it, they **live one page**.

**A page can contain:**
- one minute
- one memory
- one possible life
- one unresolved regret
- one entire symbolic lifetime

When the page turns, the life ends.

The player returns *changed*, but **not authorized to claim the page was
"real."**

**Core rule:**
> *A lived vision may be meaningful without being literal.*

## 7. Akashic Book UI

Pages do not display text first. They display a **renderer-state
header** that lets HAL classify the experience before the player enters
it.

**Example:**
```
PAGE_ID:           AK-144
IDENTITY_ANCHOR:   WEAK
TIME_DILATION:     HIGH
OBJECTHOOD_RISK:   MEDIUM
CLAIM_STATUS:      NO_CLAIM
RETURN_KEY:        BREATH + RECEIPT
```

The header is the contract: it tells you what you're about to enter
and what you'll need to come back. **No page can be entered without an
acknowledged `RETURN_KEY`.**
