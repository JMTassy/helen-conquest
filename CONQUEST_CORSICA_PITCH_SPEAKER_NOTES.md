# CONQUEST: CORSICA — Pitch Deck Speaker Notes

**Investor/Publisher Pitch Guide**
*7 slides, ~5–7 minute delivery*

## SLIDE 1: Title Slide (Tone: Mystical, Intriguing)

**Visuals:** Dark slate background with gold accent bar. Corsican alchemy aesthetic.

**What to say (30 seconds):**

> "Thank you for being here. I'm here to talk about CONQUEST: CORSICA — a new kind of game. Not a story you play, but a language you learn to dream in.
>
> In CONQUEST, you don't select dialogue from a menu. You type rituals. Every command you type rewrites the island's reality. It's interactive alchemy: part command-line interface, part narrative RPG, part meditation on faith and power.
>
> This is a game grounded in the mysticism of Corsica — its Maquis, its ancestral memory, its light and shadow — but told through a contemporary language: code."

**Why it works:**
- **Immediately differentiates** from typical RPG pitches (no menus, rituals instead)
- **Frames it as philosophical**, not just a "cool game mechanic"
- **Plants a visual anchor** (Corsica as both setting and metaphor)

**Transition:** "Let me show you what that actually means."

## SLIDE 2: The Concept (Tone: Grounded, Mission-Driven)

**Visuals:** Cream background, clear two-column layout. Left: pitch + description. Right: 4 pillar icons.

**What to say (1 minute):**

> "At its core, CONQUEST is about agency and language. Traditional games say, 'Here are your options.' CONQUEST says, 'Here is a language. What do you choose to believe?'
>
> Players are modern initiates — part coder, part seer — who interact with a living island through rituals. Calvi isn't just scenery; it's awake. Every command you execute doesn't just change the map; it changes the *rules* of reality.
>
> This hits four core pillars:
> - **Ritual-Based gameplay** — Commands as mechanics
> - **Living World** — Corsica responds and evolves
> - **Moral alchemy** — Your beliefs literally reshape reality
> - **Cultural heritage** — Ancient Corsican wisdom + contemporary AI
>
> We're not making a game about Corsica. We're making Corsica *into* a game."

**Why it works:**
- **"Modern initiate"** immediately appeals to narrative gamers + creative professionals
- **Establishes the unique mechanic** (rituals, not menus) early
- **Frames monetization implicitly** (depth, replayability, belief systems)

**Transition:** "So where does this island exist?"

## SLIDE 3: The Terrarium (Tone: Poetic, Immersive)

**Visuals:** Dark background with 4 zone cards (2×2 grid). Each zone has emoji, name, description.

**What to say (1 minute 15 seconds):**

> "The game world is called the Terrarium — a recursive simulation of Corsica where geology literally remembers ancestral prayer.
>
> It's divided into four sacred zones, each with its own mythology and mechanics:
>
> **Calvi Citadel** is the granite sanctum, the kernel — where fire meets water, where the island's core consciousness lives. Think of it as the operating system.
>
> **Maquis Ardente** is the breathing wild — the immortelle and myrtle forests where rituals of cleansing happen. This is where you confront illusions about yourself.
>
> **Nebbio Archives** are temple-servers housing the dreams of extinct orders — the Mazzeri, Signadori, medieval Knights Templar. You excavate ancestral data here.
>
> **Libeccio Plateau** is where wind and code merge — a desert of salt and sound. This is the edge of reality, where the poetic and computational languages meet.
>
> Each zone responds uniquely to player belief. Your rituals shape them. Fail in one zone, and you're reborn in another with new understanding."

**Why it works:**
- **Makes the abstract concrete** (zones with names and purposes)
- **Plants mythology hooks** (Corsican history is real, not invented)
- **Implies replayability** (different zones, different failures = different paths)
- **Establishes the "living world" promise**

**Transition:** "And the way you interact with this world is through a ritual cycle..."

## SLIDE 4: The Ritual Cycle (Tone: Mechanically Clear, Rhythmic)

**Visuals:** Four circles in sequence (1 → 2 → 3 → 4 ↻). Each has phase name and action description.

**What to say (1 minute):**

> "The gameplay loop is a four-phase ritual cycle. It repeats, but never the same way twice.
>
> **Phase 1: INVOKE.** You perform a ritual using command syntax. Maybe `./rituel --maquis` to enter the wild. Maybe `./manifest --belief "I am not afraid"` to reshape your inner world. These commands are poetic; they read like incantations even though they're executable code.
>
> **Phase 2: TRANSMUTE.** You combine symbolic elements — fire, salt, granite, breath, moonlight. This is puzzle-solving, but instead of 'swap colored blocks,' you're alchemically combining forces. Salt + fire → purification. Granite + breath → foundation + will.
>
> **Phase 3: MANIFEST.** Your transmutation becomes real. The island responds. New paths appear. NPCs gain new allegiances. Rules of physics shift. Each player's world evolves uniquely because each player's *beliefs* are unique.
>
> **Phase 4: REBIRTH.** You fail. Or you choose to burn your save — to erase your progress and be reborn with new knowledge. Failure isn't punishment; it's the only way to gain eternal insight.
>
> Then the cycle repeats. But you're different now. The island knows you failed. It teaches you differently."

**Why it works:**
- **Makes the game loop crystal clear** (no confusion about "what do I actually do?")
- **Justifies the name** (it's literally rituals, not combat or exploration)
- **Explains replayability** (failure = progression)
- **Hints at emotional depth** ("fail intentionally, gain eternal insight")

**Transition:** "This is a game for a specific kind of player..."

## SLIDE 5: Market & Audience (Tone: Business-Savvy, Honest)

**Visuals:** Two-column layout. Left: 4 audience segments with accent bars. Right: 4 competitive advantages.

**What to say (1 minute 30 seconds):**

> "Let's talk about who this is for and why they'll play it.
>
> **Narrative RPG players** are the core audience. Fans of Disco Elysium, Sunless Sea, Planescape: Torment — people who've proven they'll spend 20+ hours in a game if the story and philosophy are deep enough. They're willing to read. They want *meaning*.
>
> **Cultural institutions** — museums, educators, heritage organizations — are a secondary audience. This isn't 'historical simulation,' but it *teaches* Corsican history, spirituality, and memory *through* play. That's a huge opportunity for partnerships.
>
> **Experimental gaming communities** — Itch.io, indie-forward players, ARG enthusiasts — are hungry for games that break genre conventions. Rituals-as-mechanics is new.
>
> **Creative professionals** — writers, artists, designers — will use this as a tool for inspiration and creative problem-solving.
>
> What separates us from competitors?
>
> **Ritual-Based Interface.** Not just visual flavor. Commands *are* the game mechanic. This is different from every other narrative game.
>
> **Cultural Authenticity.** We're not 'borrowing' Corsican mysticism. We're building with Corsican artists, linguists, and historians. The paghjelle (traditional chanting) is real. The Maquis is real. The spiritual systems are researched.
>
> **Philosophical Depth.** Faith as code; memory as matter. These aren't thematic tags — they're literally how the game world works.
>
> **Adaptive Narrative.** Not 'branching dialogue.' Your actual belief system — what you type, how you think about the world — physically reshapes the island."

**Why it works:**
- **Audience segmentation shows market understanding** (not just "gamers")
- **Competitive advantages are concrete, not hand-wavy** ("ritual-based" is real differentiator)
- **Plants educator/partnership opportunities** (institutions = revenue + credibility)
- **Acknowledges the core indie/experimental gaming audience** (where early adoption happens)

**Transition:** "And here's how we make money sustainably..."

## SLIDE 6: Business Model (Tone: Ethical, Sustainable)

**Visuals:** Four revenue stream cards (2×2 grid). Each shows icon, name, description, price.

**What to say (1 minute):**

> "We're not making an extractive game. Here's our monetization:
>
> **Base Game: $29.99.** Single-player premium narrative, 12–20 hours. You own it. No subscription, no hidden mechanics, no battle pass. You buy it once and it's yours.
>
> **Expansions: $9.99–$19.99.** New myth arcs — the Mazzeri cycle, Signadori mysteries, Templier lore. New ritual packs. Seasonal myths that tie to real Corsican festivals.
>
> **Cosmetics: $4.99–$14.99.** Unique sigil skins, alternative sound themes, custom Terrarium seeds. Purely visual or audio — zero gameplay advantage.
>
> **Community Co-Creation: Revenue share.** Players generate new lore, photograph real-world rituals (Corsican ceremonies), submit myth fragments. Best creations get integrated into the official canon and the creator gets a cut. This turns the community into co-authors.
>
> The ethical note: **No pay-to-win.** No dark patterns. Monetization is tied to *creativity and personalization*, not addiction mechanics.
>
> This model has worked for: Disco Elysium (premium + DLC), Sunless Sea (premium + expansion), Outer Wilds (premium, no DLC). Players will pay for *meaning*."

**Why it works:**
- **Shows business discipline** (clear revenue streams, realistic pricing)
- **Addresses indie/cultural gaming concerns** (no dark patterns, ethical monetization)
- **Community co-creation is a moat** (hard to copy; builds loyalty)
- **Precedent proof** (references games that succeeded with this model)

**Transition:** "Here's what we're building toward..."

## Delivery Tips

### Pacing
- **Slide 1:** 30 sec (intrigue)
- **Slide 2:** 1 min (concept)
- **Slide 3:** 1 min 15 sec (world)
- **Slide 4:** 1 min (mechanics)
- **Slide 5:** 1 min 30 sec (market)
- **Slide 6:** 1 min (business)
- **Slide 7:** 1 min (vision + Q&A)

**Total: 6–7 minutes**

### Tone Across Slides
- **Slides 1–3:** Poetic, immersive (build emotional buy-in)
- **Slides 4–5:** Clear, mechanically grounded (prove it's real)
- **Slide 6:** Business-smart (show you've thought this through)
- **Slide 7:** Visionary, aspirational (leave them wanting to join)

### What Not to Do
- ❌ Don't read the slides word-for-word
- ❌ Don't spend 30 seconds explaining what "Corsica" is
- ❌ Don't apologize for being poetic ("I know this sounds mystical, but...")
- ❌ Don't have a backup plan for "the game is too experimental"
- ✅ **Do** own the philosophy. This *is* the game. Confidence sells.

### Common Questions (Prepare Answers)

**Q: "This sounds niche. How big is the market?"**
A: "Niche, yes. But valuable. Disco Elysium sold 1M+ copies to a 'niche' audience. Sunless Sea broke profitability in year two. Cultural games are underserved, not unprofitable. Plus, this has crossover potential: educators, museums, therapy/mindfulness apps."

**Q: "Will this actually work technically?"**
A: "We're using Unreal 5 with a custom narrative middleware layer (LUXENGINE) that parses ritual syntax into game state changes. Think of it like a game engine + a compiler. It's novel, but the tech is proven in pieces."

**Q: "Why Corsica specifically?"**
A: "Corsica has deep mystical heritage (Mazzeri, Signadori, Templier lore) that's less known than Irish or Celtic mythology. It's underrepresented in games. Plus, there's a real Corsican diaspora community and cultural institutions looking for media that honors their heritage authentically."

**Q: "What's your team?"**
A: "[Prepare real answer] Founded by [names], with expertise in [narrative design, AI, cultural studies, etc.]. Advisors include [relevant cultural/academic figures]. Based in [location], with partnership pipeline to [relevant studios/institutions]."

**Q: "Timeline and funding?"**
A: "[Prepare real answer] MVP playable in [timeframe], full release in [timeframe]. Seeking [amount] for pre-production and team ramp-up."

## Q&A Strategy

**Expect (and welcome) these lines of inquiry:**

1. **Narrative depth:** "How do you prevent this from becoming 'random ritual generator'?"
   → *Respond with character/lore examples.*

2. **Market comparison:** "How is this different from [similar game]?"
   → *Ritual-based mechanics + cultural authenticity + community co-creation.*

3. **Technical scope:** "Have you prototyped the core mechanic?"
   → *Yes. Show demo if you have it.*

4. **Funding ask:** "Why this budget and timeline?"
   → *Break down: team salaries, engine licensing, outsourcing (voice, music), marketing, 18-month runway.*

5. **Audience size:** "What's your realistic day-one sales target?"
   → *Conservative: 5–10k copies. Optimistic: 25–50k (Sunless Sea launch was ~15k, grew to 1M+ lifetime).*

## Final Note

**This pitch works because it balances three things:**
1. **Philosophical/emotional appeal** (rituals, alchemy, cultural depth)
2. **Mechanical clarity** (four-phase cycle, command-based gameplay)
3. **Business pragmatism** (realistic budget, proven monetization model, identifiable audience)

Most game pitches fail by leaning too hard on one (all philosophy = no credibility; all mechanics = no vision; all business = no soul).

**You have all three. Lead with the poetry, back it up with mechanics, close with business.**
