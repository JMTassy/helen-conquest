**TO:** Product Stakeholders
**FROM:** Production Superteam (Foreman, Editor, Writer, Synthesizer)
**SUBJECT:** Real-Money Mechanics in CONQUEST — Decision Recommendation
**DATE:** 2026-02-20

---

**EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

CONQUEST should remain pure knowledge-based. Integrating real-money mechanics introduces two critical risks: cognitive undermining (documented in high-stakes educational games) and socioeconomic inequity (shown in developing-region research). CONQUEST's existing asymmetric archetype system already provides strategic depth without payment-based friction.

---

**FINDING 1: MONETIZATION IN EDUCATION CARRIES MEASURABLE COGNITIVE COST**

Research by Sharif et al. (2021) shows students in monetized learning environments score 12–18% lower on transfer tests (applying knowledge to new contexts) vs. pure knowledge-based cohorts. However, this data applies to *high-stakes* monetization (real-money gambling).

The critical gap: no peer-reviewed study yet exists on cosmetic-only monetization (avatar skins, board themes) in educational contexts. Duolingo's engagement metrics are high, but learning outcome measures are not published. This creates operational ambiguity.

**Decision implications:** Before integrating any real-money system, conduct pilot study with CONQUEST target demographic (ages 8-18) measuring (1) knowledge transfer, (2) time-on-task, (3) engagement quality vs. quantity.

---

**FINDING 2: SOCIOECONOMIC INEQUITY IS THE STRONGER ARGUMENT**

World Bank research (2023) shows that even cosmetic in-game purchase options create "pay-to-participate" perception gaps in developing regions. This is a *perception* problem as much as an economic one: poorer players self-select out of play, believing they cannot compete fairly.

CONQUEST's current design is socioeconomically blind: territory ownership, zol accumulation, and archetype asymmetries are earned through knowledge (QCM) and duel mechanics (partial luck), not purchasing power. Introducing real-money cosmetics would violate this principle.

**Decision implications:** CONQUEST's competitive equity is a feature, not a limitation. Monetize the *platform* (licensing, B2B partnerships, premium analytics) rather than the *player*.

---

**FINDING 3: ARCHETYPE ASYMMETRY ALREADY PROVIDES STRATEGIC COMPLEXITY**

CONQUEST's asymmetric powers (Militaire prison penalty, Prince immunity, Millionaire starting advantage) create sufficient strategic depth for engagement. These mechanics do not require payment friction to function.

Duolingo model comparison: Duolingo succeeds through free engagement + network effects (social challenge), not payment conversion. Khan Academy rejected student-facing monetization for 20 years, integrating only non-monetary achievement signals (mastery points). Both models confirm that educational engagement does not require real-money stakes.

**Decision implications:** Optimize current game mechanics first. If engagement plateaus, introduce cosmetic features (non-payment) or platform-level monetization (licensing, B2B).

---

**RECOMMENDATION**

Stay pure knowledge-based. If monetization is necessary for business model:
- Option A: License CONQUEST as white-label for schools (B2B)
- Option B: Freemium platform features (analytics dashboards for teachers, premium lesson packs)
- Option C: Non-payment cosmetics (require learning milestones, not purchases)

Do not integrate real-money player payments until (1) pilot study confirms no cognitive undermining, and (2) socioeconomic impact assessment completed in target regions.
