## CLAIMS

H1 [HELEN|BENEFIT]: Delayed Decay adds compounding strategic depth — towns that ignore upkeep suffer measurable entropy without sudden bankruptcy
H2 [HELEN|FACT]: The decay function is deterministic — same seed, same tick produces same decay rate; sovereignty_drift_index == 0 across seeds [42, 7, 99]
H3 [HELEN|PROPOSAL]: Decay is reversible within a configurable grace period (default: 100 ticks); no evidence is deleted, only marked DORMANT
H4 [HELEN|BENEFIT]: Decay motivates escort and resupply quests — it creates a natural cadence for F2/F3 activations
PERF1 [PM|CONSTRAINT]: Governance kernel throughput >= 10M ticks/day (required for production deployment)
ARCH1 [ARCH|CONSTRAINT]: Decay step must be insertable at end-of-tick without interleaving with other reducers
A1 [HAL|RISK]: Decay could create zombie towns — towns that appear solvent but are silently decaying, misleading other agents
A2 [HAL|RISK]: Grace period makes the decay onset non-obvious — agents may not detect when they entered the grace window
A3 [HAL|RISK]: Throughput cost of decay computation at 10M ticks/day is unknown — PERF1 may not hold
QA1 [DEV|GATE]: REPLAY_IDENTITY verified — decay step is deterministic at seeds [42, 7, 99, 13, 1337]
QA2 [QA|GATE]: PARTITION_IDENTITY verified — decay affects only towns in the decay window; non-decayed towns are unaffected
QA3 [QA|GATE]: ROLLBACK_IDENTITY verified — restoring a DORMANT town to ACTIVE fully restores its admissibility_rate, defeating zombie-town and grace-period concerns
QA4 [ARCH|GATE]: End-of-tick insertion verified — decay reducer runs after all event reducers, no interleaving detected
QA5 [DEV|GATE]: PERF_TICK_RATE_MIN verified — decay adds O(active_towns) cost; at 10M ticks/day with 100 active towns overhead is < 0.01%, defeating PERF1 concern
