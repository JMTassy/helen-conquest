## Why This Matters

Most governance systems fail because they treat rules as constraints imposed from outside. People resist, work around, undermine.

Oracle Town demonstrates an alternative: **Rules as crystallized wisdom.**

When people understand:
- Why rules exist (evidence)
- What happens without them (E4 cascade)
- How they prevent failure (K3 in incidents)
- That they're grounded in theory (Byzantine foundations)

Then rules stop feeling like constraints and become **the natural way to operate**.

This is sustainable governance. Not enforced, but internalized.
