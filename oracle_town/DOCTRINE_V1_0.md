# DOCTRINE V1.0 — PERSONAL INVESTMENT GOVERNANCE

**Effective Date:** January 31, 2026  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** RATIFIED & IMMUTABLE

---

## PREAMBLE

This doctrine is extracted from 60 days of observed behavior. It is not invented. It is derived from what stabilized at equilibrium.

This document is immutable. Changes require formal amendment through three independent gates: Evidence, Conservatism, Ratification.

---

## SECTION 1: THE FOUR INVESTMENT CLASSES

### 1.1 CLASS I — GOVERNABLE

Evidence exists before commitment. Downside bounded. Reversible. Externally validatable.

**Oracle Town:** Accepts ~40%

**Reject if:** Evidence missing (K1 fail-closed)

### 1.2 CLASS II — CONDITIONALLY GOVERNABLE

Partial evidence. Some narrative. Validation delayed but possible. Asymmetric risk.

**Oracle Town:** Rejects until evidence improves

### 1.3 CLASS III — SPECULATIVE

Narrative-dominant. Timing-dependent. Irreversible. Validation only after commitment.

**Oracle Town:** Consistently rejects. Logs conscious override instead.

**Example:** €700k LE TAR wall (2026-01-31 → 2027-01-31)

### 1.4 CLASS IV — EXISTENTIAL

Identity-defining. Cannot be optimized. Cannot be justified ex ante.

**Oracle Town:** Logs as witness, not evaluator.

---

## SECTION 2: THE ACCEPTANCE RATE LAW

**Healthy governance:** 35–45% ACCEPT rate

- **Above 50%:** Under-filtering
- **Below 30%:** Over-submitting
- **In zone:** Signal integrity

This is a diagnostic, not a target.

---

## SECTION 3: THE REFUSAL DOCTRINE

A REJECT is complete. It is not provisional, not a suggestion, not an opening for negotiation.

After REJECT, ask only: "What behavior did this prevent?"

If concrete answer, system works.
If no answer, system is ceremonial.

---

## SECTION 4: THE OVERRIDE PROTOCOL

### Permitted:
- CLASS III (with logging)
- CLASS IV (with logging)

### Not permitted:
- CLASS I overrides (reclassify to CLASS III)

### Logging requirements:
- Amount at risk
- Override reason (≥50 characters)
- Review date (locked, 90+ days)
- Expected outcome
- Key assumptions

Overrides are **immutable and permanent.**

---

## SECTION 5: THE SILENCE PRINCIPLE

When INSIGHT is OFF, do nothing.

No forcing conclusions. No pretending signals exist.

Waiting is an action.

---

## SECTION 6: THE DOCTRINE CHARTER (One Page)

1. Separate conviction from legitimacy
2. Do not ask governance to bless speculation
3. Trust refusals more than enthusiasm
4. Accept ~40% acceptance as health
5. Log overrides as costs, not victories
6. Respect silence as information

---

## SECTION 7: PROTECTED (CANNOT CHANGE)

- The four classes (remain distinct)
- Acceptance rate law (35–45%)
- REJECT is complete
- Override logging mandatory
- Review dates binding
- No retroactive reinterpretation

---

## SECTION 8: CAN BE AMENDED

- Evidence thresholds
- Review date timeframes
- NPC types
- Acceptance rate target zone

**Requires:** 3 gates (Evidence + Conservatism + Ratification)

---

## SECTION 9: NPCS (VOICES WITHOUT AUTHORITY)

Four observers:
1. **AccuracyWatcher** — Did REJECTs prevent harm?
2. **SpeculationTracker** — Track CLASS III overrides
3. **PatternDetector** — Is behavior drifting?
4. **RiskAnalyzer** — What is cost of overrides?

**Key:** Zero authority. Observe only.

---

## SECTION 10: AMENDMENT PROCESS

### Gate A: Evidence
- ≥2 NPC claims
- ≥90 days observation
- ≥1 counterexample

### Gate B: Conservatism
- Why is inaction more dangerous than change?
- Default: Keep doctrine

### Gate C: Ratification
- Your vote (logged, timestamped, irreversible)
- REJECT / CONDITIONALLY ACCEPT / RATIFY

## CLOSING

This doctrine is not a promise to be perfect.

It is a commitment to be consistent.

It binds your future self to standards your present self believes in.

It will refuse you. This is correct.

The best governance is resistant to your own future convenience.
