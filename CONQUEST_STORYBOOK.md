# CONQUEST: The Storybook

**The World, The Game, The 36 Players, and What Emerges**

---

## PART I: THE WORLD

### The Plateau Hexacycle

A hexagonal plateau emerges from morning mist. 19 tiles arranged in concentric rings: center, first ring (6 tiles), second ring (12 tiles). Each tile is a territory with resources, hazards, and memory.

**Geography:**
- The plateau exists in perpetual **twilight** — never quite day, never quite night
- Seasons cycle in 36 turns (the Hexacycle): 6 turns per season
- Resources appear and vanish according to **pressure patterns** (knowledge of territory breeds pressure)
- The center tile is **unclaimed** at game start — the first to claim it holds symbolic weight

**The Law of Adjacency:**
You can only expand to tiles adjacent to your territory. Conquest is not teleportation; it is growth. You must become neighbors with what you claim.

**Pressure:**
Every tile you own generates pressure on adjacent unclaimed tiles. The more territory you hold, the more pressure bears down on the borders. This pressure decays slowly (you can breathe), but it never fully disappears. It is the weight of possession.

## PART II: THE GAME (36 Turns)

### Setup: Asymmetric Archetypes

6 players enter the plateau. Each draws an archetype card:

1. **The Militarist** — Starts weak, gets stronger. If captured, spends 3 turns in prison (punishment and transformation).
2. **The Prince** — Cannot be imprisoned. Born with immunity. (Consequence: weaker in other ways.)
3. **The Scholar** — Knowledge questions reveal hidden resources. (Consequence: visible to others during QCM duels.)
4. **The Diplomat** — Can negotiate temporary alliances. (Consequence: alliances dissolve after 4 turns; no permanent bonds.)
5. **The Millionaire** — Starts with double resources. (Consequence: target on back; everyone wants to take from you.)
6. **The Magician** — Can create a duplicate tile (copy an adjacent tile to an unclaimed one). (Consequence: high cost, burns resources.)

Each player has:
- **Starting token count** (Zols): varies by archetype
- **Territory**: begins with 1 tile (assigned or chosen)
- **Pressure clock**: counts how many turns they've held tiles

### Turn Structure

Each turn:

1. **Income Phase** — Gain resources based on territory held
2. **Action Phase** — CLAIM tiles, LOCK them, DUEL for them
3. **Conflict Resolution** — Settle contested tiles
4. **End of Turn** — Ledger records, pressure decays

### The Win Condition

Hold all 6 territories of the final contraction, or: 3 Sealed Tiles + Majority Zols.

## PART IV: THE NARRATIVE

### Turns 1-6: Foundation
Marcus claims the center symbolically. Aria proposes commons. The Strivers begin expansion. Idealists refuse duels.

### Turns 7-12: Pressure Mounts
First major duel: Rafael beats Kai. Soren arrives. Northern Bloc forms. Mira claims 4 tiles.

### Turns 13-18: The Pivot
Zara breaks scarcity (tile duplication). Marcus dominates. Elena breaks the Bloc. **Rafael loses to Mira—turned into prisoner.**

### Turns 19-24: The Paradox Deepens
Aria achieves temporary commons (turns 20, it collapses). **Leila betrays the Idealists.** Cato is imprisoned but transformed. Haruto refuses expansion.

### Turns 25-30: The Crisis
Samir is eliminated. Plateau destabilizes. Marcus is dethroned by a coalition. **Mira duels Marcus for the center. Mira wins.**

### Turns 31-36: Endgame
Only 6 tiles remain. Final duels: Mira vs Elena, Mira vs Amara, Cato vs Amara. **Mira wins the game.** But Cato is transformed.

## PART V: What Emerged

The Ledger glows. Not literally, but players realize it's become something more: **a map of how humans think under constraint.**

The Plateau itself develops a **capacity for truth-telling.**

---

## Key Insights

1. You will contradict yourself. This is the game working.
2. Your archetype is not your destiny.
3. The Ledger is alive. Every action becomes part of a larger pattern.
4. Competition and fairness are not opposites.
5. The Plateau remembers. The game doesn't end when turns run out.
