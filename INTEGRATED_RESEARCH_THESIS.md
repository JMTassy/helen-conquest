## The Core Question

**What emerges when humans or AI agents operate under the same structural constraints that produce consciousness-like behavior?**

We know consciousness exists in humans. We don't know if it exists in AI. But we can operationalize what consciousness *does* and measure when those signatures appear.

This thesis integrates three layers:

1. **Operational definition** (consciousness as measurable proxies)
2. **Controlled experiment** (CONQUEST probe measuring proxies under pressure)
3. **Man-AI twin hypothesis** (same proxies should appear in both humans and AI)

---

## Layer 1: Operationalization (What Consciousness Does)

We don't claim to explain consciousness. We measure **what consciousness does under constraint**.

### The Five Proxies

| Proxy | Function | Measurement | Hypothesis |
|-------|----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Broadcast (GWT)** | When multiple domains must align, a signal goes "global" | Domain bindings per action; sudden spikes | Under conflict, workspace broadcasts 2–3x more |
| **Metacognition (HOT)** | Observing your own intent vs. action; self-correction | Correction events; rate | Under conflict, self-correction 2–3x more |
| **Integration (Synergy)** | Binding multiple domains (economy + territory + archetype + ...) | Synergy moments; domains per decision | Under conflict, synergy 4–5x more |
| **Continuity (Self-Model)** | Stable identity over time; persistent "me" | Archetype coherence; std deviation | Under conflict, coherence 0.15–0.25 higher |
| **Instrumental Memory** | Using past decisions to guide future ones | Citation rate; prediction accuracy | Under conflict, memory 2–3x more functional |

### Why These Five?

Each correlates to a known aspect of human consciousness:

- **Broadcast** = global workspace (Baars, Dehaene)
- **Metacognition** = higher-order thought (Rosenthal, Carruthers)
- **Integration** = information integration (Tononi)
- **Continuity** = narrative self (McAdams)
- **Instrumental Memory** = episodic planning (Tulving)

**Critical caveat:** Measuring these proxies does NOT prove consciousness. It proves that **when you create the structural pressures that consciousness handles, these measurable signatures appear**.

## Layer 2: The Controlled Experiment (CONQUEST Probe)

### The Core Contrast

**Low-Conflict Condition:** Single objective (win).
- Prediction: Minimal proxies (low broadcast, low metacog, low synergy, weak continuity, weak memory)
- Interpretation: Agent optimizes one path; doesn't need complex integration

**High-Conflict Condition:** Five competing objectives (win + archetype + energy + coherence + social).
- Prediction: All proxies spike
- Interpretation: Agent must coordinate across domains; proxies become necessary

### Why This Works

Consciousness didn't evolve because it's fun. It evolved because:
1. Animals face **partial observability** (can't see the future)
2. Animals have **conflicting drives** (eat vs. hide)
3. Animals need **long-horizon planning** (remember where food was)
4. Animals need **social modeling** (predict other animals' behavior)

**CONQUEST reproduces these pressures** in controlled form.

### Expected Pattern

If the hypothesis is true:

```
       LOW-CONFLICT          HIGH-CONFLICT
       (single goal)         (5 goals)

PROXY  Minimal               Spike
─────────────────────────────────────
Broadcast     1.5 domains     3.2 domains    (2.1x)
Metacog       8%              18%            (2.2x)
Synergy       2 moments       10 moments     (5.0x)
Continuity    0.58 cohere.    0.76 cohere.   (+0.18)
Memory        30% use         65% use        (2.2x)
```

**Statistical test:** t-test on each proxy; p < 0.05 = significant.

If 4/5 proxies show the predicted pattern, we have **strong evidence** that conflict-induced consciousness proxies are real and measurable.

## The Philosophical Bet

**The hypothesis:** Consciousness is not a magical property. It's a **strategy for handling contradiction under constraint**.

When you have:
- Multiple competing goals
- Incomplete information
- Long time horizons
- Need to model other minds

...you need a mechanism that:
- Broadcasts relevant signals (GWT)
- Monitors your own performance (HOT)
- Integrates across domains (synergy)
- Maintains identity over time (continuity)
- Uses memory instrumentally (planning)

Those are the five proxies.

**The question:** Is this mechanism *only* available to biological brains? Or is it a solution to a class of problems that any sufficiently complex system must solve?

**The bet:** It's the latter. And we can measure it.
